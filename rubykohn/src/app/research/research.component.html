Full Year Honors Thesis

Design of Microcosms for Testing of Contaminant Removal


Dates:  September 2018 – May 2019

Lab:  Ismail Lab

Description:  I am designing and building flow-through microcosm tanks that model engineered treatment wetlands to study the long-term filter feeding behavior of zooplankton in natural systems. I am generating designs based on previous zooplankton and treatment wetlands research as well as chemical reactor theory and fluid mechanics. To verify design requirements, I am using tracer studies, analysis of flow regime, viability studies, and initial microcosm experiments. The results of microcosm experiments will help inform treatment wetland engineers about the capacity of zooplankton to treat microbial contaminants like E. coli and the ideal conditions for treatment.




NSF Funded Research Experience for Undergraduates (REU)





Dates: June – August 2018

Lab:  Institute for Mechatronic Systems

Description: While in Germany, I created a model of charging infrastructure for electric vehicles in residential complexes based on driver habits and charger scenarios using MATLAB. During this project I became familiar with object oriented programming in MATLAB, as well as data analysis and visualization techniques. I optimized the model to significantly reduce the energy consumption of an outdated housing complex being refurbished with a smart grid controller. I directed the research and design of the model independently in collaboration with a graduate mentor.




Environmental Engineering Research Assistant


Dates: February 2017 - May 2019

Lab:  Ismail Lab

Description:  In my role as research assistant I have designed and implemented original experiments with a team assessing the ability of zooplankton to filter E. coli from aquatic systems under varied environmental conditions. I am continuing this research through my honors thesis, building flow-through microcosm tanks that model engineered treatment wetlands to study the long-term filter feeding behavior of zooplankton in natural systems. I was awarded an undergraduate summer research fellowship in 2017; I collected water quality data of a stream running through a cattle pasture at the Smith College field station to asses diurnal impact and geographic correlation of fecal contamination, informing rechanneling of the stream. I took a leadership position by coordinating laboratory upkeep, maintaining organism cultures, and training new research assistants.




Controlled Flight Aerial Vehicle Design Project

Dates: January – May 2019

Course: Aerial Vehicle Design

Description: I am designing and fabricating a small glider capable of controlled flight with the goal of staying in the air as long as possible. I am soldering chosen components onto a PCB, programming a microcontroller using Propeller Tool to control glider components during flight, and effectively debugging both hardware and software issues. After the glider and controls have been completed, I will conduct flight tests, record pressure sensor data, and iterate the design to optimize the glide ratio and flight time.




Removal of Lead by Mushroom Mycelium

Dates: September - December 2017

Course:  Contaminants in Aquatic Systems

Description: I designed an experiment with a partner measuring the sorbtion of lead from water by powdered mycelium in collaboration with a local farmer to develop a productive use for mushroom mycelium waste. Performed batch experiments, collected data using a microwave digestor and ICP-OES, and evaluated sorbtion favorability using the Freundlich isotherm model.
